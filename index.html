About Me.


I am a Japanese data journalist based in Brooklyn, New York. I recently completed a data journalism program at Columbia University Graduate School of Journalism. I’ve worked on data stories such as the negative ecological impacts of salmon farming and how the low-income community suffers significantly more from the heatwaves in NYC. Nowadays, I code and design data stories, but I also spent years working in investment banks and a startup.

I love going on adventures, from sled dragging arctic expeditions to climbing high-altitude mountains. I am currently training for 12 day polar expedition in the largest glacier in Europe, Vatnajökull, in April 2023. I plan to collect data on the glacier, which can perhaps tell an important story about the changing climate.
